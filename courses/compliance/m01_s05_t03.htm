Thus far we've seen popups and subscreens launched from green text links. There are, however, two other methods of launching popups and subscreens: hotspots and buttons. We'll examine buttons on the next page.

A hotspot is a clearly marked area of a graphic which is selectable by the learner. Typically, the learner is instructed to select the label or labels:

Select the labels shown in the graphic to open a popup, a subscreen, and a PDF.

As mentioned previously, checkmarks are optional, as is the requirement that hotspot links be clicked before allowing the learner to move to the next screen. More on this later.
